The moving scooter on your food delivery app is three engineering systems disguised as one dot.

1. The location is never stored in a database. 300k riders sending GPS every 4 seconds is 75,000 writes per second of data that expires in 4 seconds. Fix: Kafka absorbs the stream, a processor keeps only the latest position per order in Redis, WebSockets push updates to your app. No polling. No database hotspot.

2. Raw GPS lies. On a phone in a dense city, scatter is 20 to 40 meters. The server runs a Hidden Markov Model (GraphHopper, Viterbi) to snap noisy points onto actual road segments. Your app interpolates at 60 fps between sparse 4-second truth updates. Sparse truth. Smooth fiction on the client. Decoupled on purpose.

3. "Arriving in 32 min" is four separate ML models, not one: time to assign a rider, first mile to the restaurant, kitchen prep wait, last mile to your door. When the number goes up, one model detected real traffic slowing on the rider's road, measured from the GPS of other Swiggy riders on the same streets. The fleet is the traffic sensor.

The through-line: the live path never does work you can precompute. Kafka absorbs the firehose. Road graphs are precomputed offline. ETA refreshes continuously instead of guessing once at order time.
